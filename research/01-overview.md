# pump.fun — Platform Overview & History

## What is pump.fun?

pump.fun is a Solana-based memecoin launchpad that enables anyone to create and trade tokens instantly with no coding knowledge. Launched on **January 19, 2024** by three English entrepreneurs — **Noah Tweedale, Alon Cohen, and Dylan Kerler** — the platform was built to solve the pervasive problem of rug pulls in the memecoin space.

The core value proposition: **"Fair launch" tokens with no presales, no team allocations, and automatically burned liquidity.**

## How It Works (High Level)

1. **Token Creation** — Upload image, pick name/ticker → token instantly created (1B supply, SPL/Token2022 standard)
2. **Bonding Curve Trading** — 800M tokens (80%) placed on a constant-product bonding curve. Users buy/sell directly against the curve.
3. **Graduation** — When ~85 SOL is raised (~$69K market cap), the token auto-migrates to PumpSwap DEX
4. **Post-Graduation** — Tokens trade on PumpSwap with standard AMM mechanics. LP tokens are burned permanently.

## The May 2024 Exploit

A former employee identified as **"STACCoverflow" (Jarett Dunn)** used privileged access keys to:
- Used flash loans on Raydium to buy tokens to 100% on bonding curves
- Drained liquidity from bonding curve contracts
- Stole ~$1.9M (12,300 SOL) from ~$45M in contracts
- Platform paused trading temporarily
- **All affected users were reimbursed 100%**
- Attacker was arrested in the UK
- He posted on X: *"about to change the course of history. n then rot in jail"*

## The LIBRA Scandal (Feb 2025)

The LIBRA token, promoted by Argentine President Javier Milei, launched on pump.fun:
- Reached **$4.4 billion market cap** at peak
- Insiders siphoned **$107M in liquidity**
- Token crashed **95% in 48 hours**
- Led to political scrutiny and further regulatory attention
